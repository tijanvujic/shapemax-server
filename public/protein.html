Protein & Muscle Growth

Protein is essential for muscle growth, recovery, and overall health. It helps repair muscle fibers and supports metabolism.

How Much Protein Do You Need?

  • Sedentary individuals: 0.8g per kg of body weight
  • Active individuals: 1.2g - 2.0g per kg of body weight
  • Strength training athletes: 1.6g - 2.2g per kg of body weight
  • Endurance athletes: 1.2g - 1.4g per kg of body weight

Use Our Protein Calculator to determine your daily protein requirement.

High-Protein Meal Ideas

  • Breakfast: Scrambled eggs with spinach & whole-grain toast
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with quinoa
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with roasted vegetables
  • Snack: Greek yogurt with almonds & honey

Benefits of Protein for Lifters

  • Muscle Repair: Repairs microtears in muscle tissue after intense workouts.
  • Strength Gains: Supports hypertrophy and strength adaptation.
  • Satiety: Helps reduce cravings and supports fat loss during cuts.
  • Hormonal Health: Key amino acids support testosterone and growth hormone levels.
  • Metabolism: Boosts calorie burn through the thermic effect of food (TEF).
